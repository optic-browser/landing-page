Performance-first, surveillance-last.

Firefox ESR Core

Optic starts from Firefox ESR so the browser inherits Mozilla's long-term security cadence, stability, and native privacy primitives.

Mercury-Inspired Performance

Build tuning and compiler flags are studied and applied where they improve speed without compromising trust.

Privacy via user.js

Firefox's preference system lets Optic ship opinionated defaults like RFP, Total Cookie Protection, and DNS over HTTPS without patching core source.

One browser. Four levels of control.

Choose a profile in seconds and fine-tune every control independently. uBlock Origin is preinstalled, Firefox-native Total Cookie Protection is on, and DNS over HTTPS is part of the privacy stack.

Surveillance pricing is likely coming fast.

As data models get better, websites and platforms can charge different prices to different people based on risk scores, urgency signals, location, or device profile. Two users can see different offers for the same product.

How surveillance pricing works

Fingerprints, behavioral signals, and cross-site profiles can feed predictive models that decide who sees discounts and who gets the expensive version first.

How Optic helps

Optic reduces entropy, partitions state, and minimizes leaked metadata, making profiling less reliable and personalized overcharging harder to enforce.

About Us

Optic began with a simple idea: privacy tools should feel powerful and calm, not intimidating. We are building a browser where people can browse faster, understand what is being collected, and tune protection level by level.

Our vision

From the project spec and Firefox edition notes: privacy by default, clear controls, long-term security updates, and elegant design that does not feel like a security console. The goal is control without friction.

Why the name "Optic"

The name reflects clarity. Browsing should feel like focusing a lens: less noise, clearer signals, and full awareness of what the web can see.

Logo inspiration: LP01 fiber optic cable

The concentric-ring logo is inspired by the layered cross-section profile of an LP01 fiber optic mode. It symbolizes guided light, precise routing, and stable transmission - the same principles we want for private browsing.

Optic is building a calmer web.

Faster by design, private by default, and transparent all the way down.
