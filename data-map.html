Who Collects What (and from where)

This map summarizes documented collection behavior from official policies. Always read full policy text before making legal or compliance decisions.

Collection map

Table format: company, typical data categories, and source path.

Company What they say they collect Source path
Google Account data, activity history, location signals, app/browser/device identifiers, activity on partner sites/apps, and ad interaction data. User-provided data, automatic device/service logging, cookies/local storage, trusted/public/partner sources.
Microsoft Data from direct interactions, product usage, diagnostics, account and contact data, cookies/beacons, and some third-party data. Direct user input, product telemetry, partner/vendor integrations, affiliates/subsidiaries, and ad/analytics systems.
TikTok Profile/account data, user content and metadata, device/network fingerprints, usage and ad interaction data, contacts, and location. User-provided content, automatic collection, advertisers/publishers and measurement partners, third-party account links, and public sources.
LinkedIn Profile and employment data, service usage logs, ad interaction data, device IDs/IP, partner-provided professional data, and inferred traits. User profile/posts, others' uploads and mentions, partner/customer feeds, cookies/pixels/ad tags, and cross-service integrations.
OpenAI Account details, prompts/content uploads, log and usage data, device and location approximations, cookies, and selected third-party/public data. User input, service interaction logs, optional connected services, security/marketing partners, and public internet data for model work.
Snap Account/profile data, content interactions, ad identifiers, device and sensor details, location, contact graph, and advertiser-linked signals. User-provided data, generated usage/content metadata, cookies/pixels, linked third-party services, advertisers, affiliates, and other users.

How to use this map

Use it as a decision layer: if a service pulls data from many external sources, isolate it in a separate browser profile and reduce granted app permissions.

Control starts with visibility.

You cannot reduce exposure you cannot see. Map first, then choose mitigations.
